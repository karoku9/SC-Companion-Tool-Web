# v0.22 Expanded Universe Data

## Release goal

Make ordinary hauling destinations resolve through one maintainable location model before Game.log intake is introduced. A parsed name must map to a stable operational destination, hierarchy, in-game navigation target, source record, service/risk profile and Starmap anchor without inventing precision or live telemetry.

## Delivered coverage

- 130 normalized location records.
- 84 operational destinations: 80 in Stanton, 3 in Pyro and 1 in Nyx.
- 34 spaceports, orbital or Lagrange stations, gateways and interstellar destinations.
- 43 Stanton mining, research, agricultural or industrial outposts.
- 7 Stanton distribution centers and logistics complexes.
- Existing Checkmate, Orbituary, Ruin Station and Levski coverage retained.
- Punctuation, spacing and compact aliases for station and field-location names.

## Complete Location Intel

- Every operational destination answers twelve service categories.
- Risk, fuel/repair, food/drink and medical availability appear first.
- Static danger is destination-specific and separate from onboard-cargo exposure.
- Ordinary outposts distinguish pad, fuel, vehicle and commodity facilities from absent food, medical care or habitation.
- Distribution centers emphasize freight infrastructure while public amenities remain limited.
- Outlaw stations and surface sites remain distinct from controlled corporate facilities.

## Data boundaries

- Official RSI references remain marked as official.
- Reviewed community location pages, field-location reviews and unpacked game-data sources remain identifiable.
- Every record carries a game version and review date.
- Physical body mappings, schematic offsets, Lagrange anchors, gateway anchors and surface anchors have distinct geometry statuses.
- Surface anchors are tied to the correct parent planet or moon but do not claim surveyed coordinates.
- Registry presence never implies live traffic, player activity, comm-array state, security, market stock or service uptime.
- Service availability never implies that the approach is safe.

## Validation contract

- Unique source and location IDs.
- Every parent, source and anchor body must exist.
- Every operational destination must expose a navigation target and map anchor.
- Exact and common punctuation variants must resolve predictably.
- Every anchor must produce finite map and distance coordinates.
- Every destination must expose twelve service answers and non-unknown static risk.
- Mission parsing must reuse the same resolver and avoid custom locations for supported aliases.
- Node and Chromium workflows must remain green.
- Station, outpost, distribution-center and mobile screenshots must be inspected before merge.

## Completed verification

- All Node, static-contract and JavaScript syntax checks passed.
- Chromium exercised interstellar stations plus Checkmate, ARC-L2, Grim HEX, Teasa, HDMS-Bezdek, S4LD01 and Bud’s Growery without browser errors.
- Mission review, Planner, Operations, Location Intel, Itinerary, Stanton System and Network views were exercised.
- Desktop, tablet and mobile artifacts were manually inspected for hierarchy, readability, label collisions and horizontal overflow.
- The combined registry resolves `HDMS Bezdek`, `Rayari Deltana`, `Buds Growery` and `S4LD01` through the same search and mission-validation path.
